Your week, and how it actually felt.

Most planners tell you what you meant to do. Daybook keeps your schedule, to-dos, journal, and goals together, then lines them up against your sleep and your mood — so you can see which kinds of days work for you.

What’s inside

  • Schedule

    Block out the week by dragging on the grid. Colour-code by what kind of time it is, and rate how each activity actually felt.

  • To-Do

    Two kinds of task, because they aren’t the same thing: hard deadlines, and the days you plan to do the work. Plans can point at the deadline they serve.

  • Journal

    A short check-in each morning and night, plus somewhere to write freely when a day needs more than a number.

  • Goals

    Set a target in the shape you actually think in — once every three days, twice a week, three hours — and see how long it’s been.

  • Sleep & Workouts

    Bring in your Oura sleep scores and your Strava workouts, so the record of your week includes the parts you didn’t plan.
